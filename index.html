The W.i.A? Institute is a project about the word Art – who uses it, who benefits from it, who has defined it, and how we will continue to use and define it in the future. As cultural organizer, La Tanya Autry says, “Museums are not neutral.” She is right. Neither is the word art. Access to the ideas and ownership of art start with access to the word itself.

This initiative serves as an aggregate of projects of whose medium are voices. Voices will be expressed in different forms, although sometimes literal voices, collected through projects that begin to assemble a qualitative understanding and democratic reckoning of what art is as voiced by everyone.

 

PROJECTS

Overview:

Projects are grouped in a number of classifications often garnering multiple classification tags. As a research-focused project-based institute we try to make upcoming projects available ahead of time and always archive finished projects. Classification tags help to inform users of the nature of a given project at a glance.


 
↳ CLASSIFICATION(S)

ACTIVE

Active projects are actively being expanded or are actively participatory. These projects funded and no longer in development.

PARTICIPATORY

Participatory projects are projects that necessitate participation to exist. Their medium is response and their collaborators are what make these projects possible. If you see a an active project with a participatory tag look for how you can add your voice.

IN DEVELOPMENT

Projects that are in development are fully formed conceptually but not technically or financially. They represent ideas that are looking for a home or the right collaborators.

OBSERVATIONAL

Observational projects are projects that have aggregated qualitative data to be presented. While the presented data may be the formalization of participants they are no longer participatory.

ARCHIVED

Archived projects are simple projects that are complete. They are no longer active. They may have been participatory at one point but are no longer.
